Exercise







- Benefits of regular physical activities

- Good exercises to do every day

Want to feel better and have more energy? and even add years to your life?
you just need to exercise!!





Here are some Benefits of regular physical activities:

  1. Exercise controls weight
  2. Exercise combats health conditions and diseases
  3. Exercise improve brain function and protect the memory
  4. Exercise strengthens muscles and bones
  5. Exercise improves mood
  6. Exercise promotes relaxation and better sleep
  7. Exercise boosts energy

Here are some physical activities:




Beginner Routine:
Duration: 30-45 minutes

type of exercise Time taken What to do?
Warm-Up 5-10 minutes
  • 5 minutes of brisk walking or light jogging
  • Dynamic stretches: arm circles, leg swings, torso twists
Strength Training 20-25 minutes
  • weight squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Push-ups (can be modified): 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Body weight lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps each leg
  • Plank: 3 sets, hold for 20-30 seconds each
  • Dumbbell rows (if available): 3 sets of 8-10 reps
Cardio 10-15 minutes
  • Alternating between jogging and walking for 10-15 minutes
  • Option: Jumping jacks, high knees, or cycling if available
Cool Down 5-10 minutes
  • Stretching: focusing on major muscle groups, holding each stretch for 15-30 seconds



Intermediate Routine:
Duration: 45-60 minutes

type of exercise Time taken What to do?
Warm-Up 10 minutes
  • 5 minutes of light jogging or cycling
  • Dynamic stretches: leg swings, arm circles, hip circles
Strength Training 25-30 minutes
  • Goblet squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Push-ups (regular or incline): 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Dumbbell lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps each leg
  • Dumbbell chest press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Bent-over dumbbell rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
Cardio 15-20 minutes
  • Interval training: alternate between high-intensity bursts (e.g., sprinting) and lower-intensity periods (e.g., jogging or brisk walking) for 15-20 minutes
Cool Down 10 minutes
  • Stretching: focusing on all major muscle groups, holding each stretch for 20-30 seconds



Advanced Routine:
Duration: 60-75 minutes

type of exercise Time taken What to do?
Warm-Up 5-10 minutes
  • 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, cycling, or jump rope)
  • Dynamic stretching: leg swings, arm circles, hip circles, torso twists
Strength Training 30-40 minutes
  • Barbell squats: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Deadlifts: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Bench press: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Pull-ups or chin-ups: 4 sets to failure
  • Barbell rows: 4 sets of 8-10 reps
Cardio 20-30 minutes
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT) incorporating various exercises such as sprints, burpees, mountain climbers, and kettlebell swings for 20-30 minutes
Cool Down 10-15 minutes
  • Static stretching: focusing on all major muscle groups, holding each stretch for 30-45 seconds
  • Foam rolling or self-myofascial release



Expert Routine:
Duration: 75-90 minutes

type of exercise Time taken What to do?
Warm-Up 12 minutes
  • Full-body dynamic warm-up including movements such as jumping lunges, inchworms, lateral shuffles, and arm circles for 12 minutes.
Strength Training 40-45 minutes
  • Squat variations (back squats, front squats, overhead squats): 5 sets of 5 reps each.
  • Deadlift variations (conventional deadlifts, sumo deadlifts, deficit deadlifts): 5 sets of 5 reps each.
  • Weighted pull-ups or muscle-ups: 5 sets of 5 reps.
  • Barbell or dumbbell bench press: 5 sets of 5 reps.
  • Turkish get-ups: 3 sets of 3 reps per side.
Cardio 25-30 minutes
  • Tabata training: 8 rounds of 20 seconds of maximum effort followed by 10 seconds of rest for exercises like sprinting, rowing, or cycling for 25-30 minutes.
Cool Down 12 minutes
  • Comprehensive static stretching routine targeting all major muscle groups, holding each stretch for 45-60 seconds.
  • Using foam roller, lacrosse ball, or massage gun for myofascial release.
  • Mindful breathing exercises or guided meditation to enhance recovery and relaxation.
